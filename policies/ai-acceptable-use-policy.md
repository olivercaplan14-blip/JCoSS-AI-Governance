# JCoSS Artificial Intelligence — Acceptable Use Policy

**Document owner:** JCoSS IT & Governance Team
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective date:** September 2024
**Review date:** September 2025

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out the principles and rules that govern how students, staff, and governors of JCoSS (Jeannette Cohen School of Solihull) may use Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools and technologies within school activities. It aims to:

- support safe, responsible, and ethical use of AI;
- protect the privacy and wellbeing of all members of the school community;
- uphold academic integrity and the school's values.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- all students in Years 7–13;
- all teaching and support staff;
- governors and volunteers acting on behalf of JCoSS;
- any AI tools accessed on school devices, school networks, or for school-related work on personal devices.

---

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **AI tool** | Any software that uses machine learning or large language models to generate, summarise, translate, or otherwise process text, images, code, or other content (e.g. ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, Google Gemini, Grammarly AI). |
| **Generative AI** | AI capable of producing new content (text, images, audio, etc.) in response to a prompt. |
| **Academic work** | Any assignment, assessment, coursework, homework, or examination task submitted for school credit. |

---

## 4. Permitted Uses

### 4.1 Students

Students **may** use approved AI tools to:

- explore concepts, generate ideas, and support learning under teacher direction;
- obtain feedback on drafts before final submission, provided the AI's contribution is acknowledged;
- access accessibility features (e.g. text-to-speech, spelling assistance).

### 4.2 Staff

Staff **may** use AI tools to:

- assist with lesson planning, resource creation, and administrative tasks;
- provide personalised feedback drafts, subject to professional review before sharing with students;
- undertake continuing professional development related to AI in education.

---

## 5. Prohibited Uses

The following are prohibited for all users:

1. Submitting AI-generated content as one's own original work in graded assessments without explicit teacher permission and acknowledgement.
2. Using AI tools to impersonate another person or to create misleading, harmful, or offensive content.
3. Inputting personally identifiable information (PII) about students, staff, or third parties into any AI tool not authorised by the school.
4. Using AI to circumvent safeguarding controls, parental controls, or school filtering systems.
5. Accessing AI tools that have been explicitly blocked by the school's network policy.

---

## 6. Academic Integrity

Where AI assistance is permitted, students must:

- disclose the use of any AI tool in a brief acknowledgement statement appended to their work (e.g. *"AI tool used: [Name], purpose: [summarise sources / draft outline]"*);
- be able to discuss and explain all content they submit;
- ensure the final work reflects their own understanding and effort.

Failure to disclose AI use where it was permitted, or submitting AI-generated work where it was not permitted, will be treated as an academic integrity violation under the school's Behaviour Policy.

---

## 7. Data Protection

- No student personal data (names, year groups, SEN information, etc.) may be entered into any third-party AI tool without prior written approval from the Data Protection Officer (DPO).
- Staff using school-approved AI tools integrated into Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace must comply with the school's Data Protection Policy.
- All AI tools in use must be assessed via the school's Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) process before deployment.

---

## 8. Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **Students** | Follow this policy; acknowledge AI use; report concerns to their teacher. |
| **Teaching staff** | Communicate permitted AI use clearly for each task; model responsible AI use. |
| **Head of IT** | Maintain the approved list of AI tools; conduct DPIAs; review network access controls. |
| **SLT** | Provide strategic direction; ensure adequate training; review this policy annually. |
| **DPO** | Advise on data protection obligations relating to AI use. |

---

## 9. Breaches

Breaches of this policy will be handled in accordance with the school's Behaviour Policy (students) or Disciplinary Procedure (staff). Potential consequences include, but are not limited to, restricted access to technology, loss of assessment marks, and formal disciplinary action.

---

## 10. Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by the Head of IT in consultation with the Senior Leadership Team, or sooner if significant changes in AI technology or legislation require it.
