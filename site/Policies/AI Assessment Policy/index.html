    Artificial Intelligence Assessment Policy

    Field Details
    Document Owner Oliver Caplan
    Version 1.0
    Status Draft
    Review Frequency Annual
    Classification Public

    1. Purpose

    This policy defines how Artificial Intelligence (AI) may be used in student assessment.

    The school recognises that AI technologies are becoming increasingly common in education and the workplace. Assessment practices should therefore encourage students to develop the ability to use AI responsibly while ensuring that submitted work accurately represents their own knowledge, understanding and skills.

    The purpose of this policy is to maintain academic integrity while supporting modern approaches to learning.

    2. Scope

    This policy applies to:

    • Homework
    • Coursework
    • Controlled assessments
    • Projects
    • Presentations
    • Extended writing tasks
    • Any other assessed student work

    It applies to all students across the school.

    3. Principles

    3.1 Student understanding must remain central

    Assessment should measure the student's own:

    • Knowledge
    • Understanding
    • Reasoning
    • Creativity
    • Problem-solving ability
    • Ability to communicate ideas

    AI may support learning but must not replace the student's own contribution.

    3.2 AI use should be transparent

    Where students use AI as part of their work, they should follow teacher guidance regarding acknowledgement.

    Students may be required to record:

    • The AI tool used.
    • The purpose of its use.
    • The prompts or instructions provided.
    • How the output was modified or evaluated.

    3.3 AI should support, not complete, assessed work

    Appropriate AI use may include:

    • Explaining difficult concepts.
    • Suggesting possible approaches.
    • Providing feedback on drafts.
    • Helping identify areas for improvement.
    • Supporting revision.

    AI use is inappropriate where it:

    • Produces the majority of submitted work.
    • Replaces independent thinking.
    • Generates answers that the student cannot explain.
    • Misrepresents the student's ability.

    4. Categories of AI Use in Assessment

    Category 1: AI prohibited

    AI must not be used.

    Examples:

    • Examinations.
    • Assessments explicitly completed under controlled conditions.
    • Tasks where independent performance is being measured.

    Category 2: AI permitted with acknowledgement

    AI may be used as a support tool.

    Examples:

    • Brainstorming ideas.
    • Improving structure.
    • Generating practice materials.
    • Receiving feedback.

    Students must acknowledge their use where requested.

    Category 3: AI encouraged

    AI may form part of the learning process.

    Examples:

    • Computer science projects.
    • Research tasks.
    • Digital literacy activities.
    • Evaluating AI-generated information.

    Students are expected to demonstrate critical judgement.

    5. Student Responsibilities

    Students must:

    • Follow teacher instructions regarding AI use.
    • Be honest about when AI has been used.
    • Ensure submitted work reflects their own understanding.
    • Verify information produced by AI.
    • Maintain records of AI assistance when required.

    Students must not:

    • Submit AI-generated content as entirely their own.
    • Use AI to complete assessments where prohibited.
    • Create false evidence of learning.
    • Use AI to avoid developing required skills.

    6. Teacher Responsibilities

    Teachers should:

    • Provide clear expectations for AI use in assessments.
    • Consider how assessment design can promote genuine understanding.
    • Explain when AI is permitted or prohibited.
    • Consider the limitations of AI detection tools.
    • Focus on evidence of student understanding rather than relying solely on detection software.

    7. AI Detection

    The school recognises that AI detection technologies are not always reliable.

    AI detection tools should not be used as the sole evidence of academic misconduct.

    Concerns regarding AI use should be investigated through appropriate methods, including:

    • Discussion with the student.
    • Review of drafts and development history.
    • Comparison with previous work.
    • Evaluation of the student's understanding.

    8. Coursework and Extended Projects

    For coursework and extended projects, students may be required to maintain evidence of their development process.

    This may include:

    • Draft versions.
    • Research notes.
    • Planning documents.
    • Reflections.
    • Records of AI assistance.

    The purpose of this evidence is to demonstrate learning and authenticity.

    9. Artificial Intelligence and Examinations

    All examination regulations and awarding body requirements take priority over this policy.

    Students must follow examination rules regarding technology and AI use.

    AI must not be used in any examination environment unless explicitly permitted by the relevant awarding organisation.

    10. Supporting AI Literacy

    The school aims to develop students who can use AI effectively and responsibly.

    Students should learn:

    • How AI systems generate outputs.
    • How to evaluate AI responses.
    • How to identify errors and bias.
    • How to use AI ethically.
    • When human judgement is required.

    11. Review

    This policy will be reviewed annually by the AI Governance Group.

    Reviews will consider:

    • Changes in AI technology.
    • Department for Education guidance.
    • Examination board requirements.
    • Feedback from students and staff.

    12. Commitment

    The school believes that the future of education requires students to understand how to work alongside AI responsibly.

    Assessment should not simply attempt to prevent AI use. Instead, it should develop students who can use technology ethically while demonstrating independent knowledge, creativity and critical thinking.
