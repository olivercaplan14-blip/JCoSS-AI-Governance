    1. Purpose

    This document defines the principles that guide the responsible adoption and use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) within the school.

    The school recognises that AI technologies are rapidly changing education, employment and society. The purpose of this statement is to encourage innovation while ensuring that AI is used safely, ethically and in a way that supports the school's educational values.

    The school does not seek to prohibit AI use. Instead, it aims to develop AI literacy, critical thinking and responsible digital citizenship among students and staff.

    2. Scope

    This statement applies to:

    • Students
    • Teaching staff
    • Administrative staff
    • Leadership teams
    • External partners using AI systems on behalf of the school

    It applies to all AI technologies used for educational, administrative or operational purposes.

    3. Definition of Artificial Intelligence

    For the purpose of this statement, Artificial Intelligence refers to computer systems capable of performing tasks that traditionally require human intelligence.

    This includes, but is not limited to:

    • Generative AI systems
    • AI-powered educational tools
    • Automated decision-support systems
    • Machine learning applications
    • AI assistants

    4. Core Principles

    4.1 AI should enhance education

    AI should be used to improve learning experiences, accessibility, creativity and productivity.

    AI should support teachers and students rather than replace human expertise, judgement or relationships.

    4.2 Human oversight is essential

    All significant decisions involving education, safeguarding or student outcomes must remain subject to human judgement.

    AI-generated information must be reviewed before being relied upon.

    4.3 Responsible innovation

    The school encourages exploration of emerging technologies where they provide educational value.

    Innovation should be balanced with consideration of:

    • Safety
    • Privacy
    • Accuracy
    • Equality
    • Educational impact

    4.4 Academic integrity

    AI must be used in a way that maintains honesty and fairness.

    Students remain responsible for demonstrating their own knowledge, understanding and skills.

    The use of AI to misrepresent work as entirely original is not acceptable.

    4.5 Privacy and security

    AI systems must be used in accordance with data protection requirements.

    Users must not input confidential, personal or safeguarding information into unauthorised AI systems.

    The school will assess privacy and security considerations before adopting new AI technologies.

    4.6 Transparency

    The school will promote openness regarding the use of AI.

    Where AI contributes significantly to work, teaching materials or decision-making processes, this should be acknowledged where appropriate.

    4.7 Fairness and accessibility

    AI should be used to support inclusion and accessibility.

    The school recognises that AI systems may contain bias or produce inaccurate information. Users must evaluate AI outputs critically.

    5. Responsibilities

    School Leadership

    The leadership team is responsible for:

    • Establishing appropriate AI governance arrangements.
    • Ensuring compliance with relevant guidance and legislation.
    • Supporting responsible innovation.

    Staff

    Staff are responsible for:

    • Using AI appropriately.
    • Checking AI-generated content for accuracy.
    • Protecting confidential information.
    • Modelling responsible AI behaviour.

    Students

    Students are responsible for:

    • Using AI honestly.
    • Following classroom and assessment guidance.
    • Evaluating AI-generated information critically.
    • Respecting privacy and safeguarding requirements.

    6. Commitment

    The school believes that AI represents an important opportunity for improving education when used responsibly.

    Our aim is to prepare students and staff for an increasingly AI-enabled world while protecting academic standards, privacy and the importance of human expertise.

    AI should be viewed as a tool that strengthens education, not as a replacement for the creativity, judgement and relationships that define effective learning.
