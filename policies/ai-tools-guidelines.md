# JCoSS Artificial Intelligence — Staff & Student Tools Guidelines

**Document owner:** Head of IT
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective date:** September 2024
**Review date:** September 2025

---

## 1. Purpose

These guidelines help staff and students understand **which AI tools are approved** for use at JCoSS and **how to use them responsibly and effectively**.

---

## 2. Approved AI Tools

The following tools have been assessed and approved for use by JCoSS. Any tool **not** on this list requires DPO and IT approval before use (see Section 5).

| Tool | Permitted Uses | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|

## 3. Guidance for Staff

### 3.1 Using AI in Lesson Planning

- AI can assist in generating lesson ideas, differentiated resources, and assessment questions.
- All AI-generated content must be **reviewed and edited** by the teacher before use; never use raw AI output without checking it for accuracy and appropriateness.
- Do not include student names or identifying information in prompts.

### 3.2 Using AI for Feedback and Assessment

- AI may be used to help draft written feedback, but the teacher must personalise and verify the feedback before sharing with students.
- Do not use AI to make high-stakes assessment decisions (e.g. predicted grades) without human review.

### 3.3 Using AI for Administrative Tasks

- AI tools can help draft routine communications, meeting notes, and reports.
- Always review AI-generated documents for accuracy; the human author remains responsible for the content.

### 3.4 Professional Development

- Staff are encouraged to explore AI tools as part of CPD. The school will provide training sessions each year.
- Queries about AI use can be directed to the Head of IT or the school's Digital Lead.

---

## 4. Guidance for Students

### 4.1 Getting Started

1. Log in with your **school Microsoft or Google account** only — never use a personal account.
2. Read the teacher's instructions carefully before using any AI tool; not all tasks permit AI assistance.
3. Remember that AI can make mistakes — always cross-check important information with reliable sources.

### 4.2 Using AI Ethically

- Think of AI as a **thinking partner**, not a replacement for your own effort.
- Do not paste entire questions, exam papers, or copyrighted text into an AI tool.
- Acknowledge AI use when required (e.g. "I used Microsoft Copilot to generate an initial outline, which I then rewrote and expanded").

### 4.3 Staying Safe Online

- Never share personal information (full name, address, phone number, etc.) in an AI chat.
- If an AI tool produces content that is offensive, disturbing, or inappropriate, close it immediately and tell your teacher.
- Report any technical issues or concerns to IT Support or your teacher.

---

## 5. Requesting Approval for a New AI Tool

If you believe a new AI tool should be considered for school use:

1. Complete the **AI Tool Proposal Form** (available from the IT office or the school intranet).
2. Submit it to the Head of IT.
3. The Head of IT will conduct a DPIA and security review within 10 school days.
4. You will be notified of the outcome. Approved tools will be added to Section 2 of this document.

---

## 6. Reporting Concerns

Report any concerns about AI use — including suspected misuse, inappropriate AI-generated content, or data breaches — to:

- **Students:** your form tutor or Head of Year
- **Staff:** the Head of IT or the DPO
- **Urgent safeguarding concerns:** the Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)

---

## 7. Review

These guidelines will be reviewed annually, or sooner if new tools are approved or significant issues arise.
