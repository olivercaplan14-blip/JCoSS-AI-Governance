# JCoSS Artificial Intelligence — Data Privacy Policy

**Document owner:** Data Protection Officer (DPO)
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective date:** September 2024
**Review date:** September 2025

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy explains how JCoSS manages personal data when using AI tools and services, ensuring compliance with the **UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)** and the **Data Protection Act 2018**.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all AI tools and services used by JCoSS staff, students, and governors, whether accessed on school-owned devices or on personal devices for school purposes.

---

## 3. Lawful Basis for Processing

JCoSS will only use AI tools that process personal data under an appropriate lawful basis:

| Scenario | Lawful Basis |
|----------|-------------|
| Staff using AI-assisted tools integrated within contracted school software (e.g. Microsoft 365 Copilot) | Legitimate interests / contract performance |
| Analysing anonymised assessment data to inform teaching | Legitimate interests |
| Processing special category data (e.g. SEN, medical) via AI tools | Explicit consent or substantial public interest |

No personal data will be entered into a consumer AI service (e.g. public ChatGPT) without a completed DPIA and approval from the DPO.

---

## 4. Data Minimisation and Anonymisation

- Staff must **anonymise or pseudonymise** personal data before using it with any AI tool wherever technically possible.
- Student names, ID numbers, and other direct identifiers must be removed before data is processed by an AI system that sends data outside the school's controlled environment.
- AI tools must not be used to re-identify individuals from anonymised datasets.

---

## 5. Data Retention

- Outputs generated by AI tools that contain personal data must be stored only as long as necessary and in line with the school's Records Retention Schedule.
- AI tools that store conversation history or model the user's data must have their data retention settings reviewed and, where possible, set to the shortest available period.

---

## 6. Third-Party AI Providers

Before any new AI tool is deployed:

1. The Head of IT must complete a **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)**.
2. A **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** must be in place with the provider.
3. The DPO must confirm that the provider offers adequate data protection guarantees, particularly for any international data transfers.
4. The tool must be added to the **Approved AI Tools Register** (maintained by IT).

---

## 7. Student Data

- Student data is subject to additional protection in line with the school's safeguarding obligations.
- AI tools must not be used to profile individual students in ways that could adversely affect their educational opportunities.
- Parents/guardians will be informed via the school's annual Data Protection Notice if AI tools process their child's personal data.

---

## 8. Security

- All approved AI tools must meet the school's minimum security standard (ISO 27001 alignment or equivalent).
- Staff must not share login credentials for AI tools.
- Any suspected data breach involving an AI tool must be reported to the DPO within **24 hours** of discovery.

---

## 9. Subject Access Requests

If a student, parent, or staff member makes a Subject Access Request (SAR) that may involve data processed by an AI tool, the Head of IT and DPO must assess whether AI-generated outputs about the individual are held and whether they form part of the response.

---

## 10. Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **DPO** | Approve AI tools; maintain DPIAs; advise on data protection obligations. |
| **Head of IT** | Maintain the Approved AI Tools Register; complete DPIAs; enforce technical controls. |
| **All staff** | Comply with this policy; report concerns or breaches promptly. |
| **SLT** | Ensure adequate resource for compliance; sign off on DPIAs where required. |

---

## 11. Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by the DPO, or sooner in response to significant changes in legislation, guidance from the ICO, or new AI tool deployments.
