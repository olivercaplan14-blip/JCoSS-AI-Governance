    1. Introduction

    Artificial Intelligence (AI) provides opportunities to improve teaching, learning and administrative processes. When used responsibly, AI can support staff by reducing repetitive tasks, improving accessibility and enabling more personalised learning experiences.

    However, AI systems do not replace professional expertise, subject knowledge or teacher judgement.

    This guidance outlines how staff should use AI safely, effectively and ethically within the school.

    2. Principles for Staff Use

    Staff using AI should follow these principles:

    Human judgement remains essential

    AI-generated content must always be reviewed before use.

    Staff remain responsible for:

    • Accuracy of information.
    • Suitability of resources.
    • Educational quality.
    • Compliance with school policies.

    AI should support professional judgement, not replace it.

    Protect student information

    Staff must protect confidential information when using AI tools.

    Staff must not enter:

    • Student names.
    • Student performance data.
    • Medical information.
    • Safeguarding information.
    • Personal circumstances.
    • Confidential school documents.

    into unauthorised AI systems.

    Use approved systems

    Only school-approved AI tools should be used for processing school-related information.

    Before using a new AI system, staff should consider:

    • Data protection requirements.
    • Security risks.
    • Educational value.
    • Accuracy and reliability.

    3. Appropriate Uses of AI

    AI may support staff in areas including:

    Lesson Planning

    AI may assist with:

    • Generating lesson ideas.
    • Creating lesson structures.
    • Suggesting activities.
    • Producing examples.
    • Adapting explanations for different abilities.

    Staff should adapt all AI-generated materials to suit their students.

    Resource Creation

    AI may help create:

    • Worksheets.
    • Revision materials.
    • Question banks.
    • Examples.
    • Classroom activities.
    • Differentiated resources.

    All resources must be checked for:

    • Accuracy.
    • Curriculum alignment.
    • Appropriate difficulty.
    • Bias or unsuitable content.

    Supporting Inclusion

    AI may assist with:

    • Simplifying explanations.
    • Creating alternative formats.
    • Supporting accessibility.
    • Producing additional learning materials.

    AI should be used to improve access to learning while maintaining appropriate challenge.

    Administrative Support

    AI may assist with:

    • Drafting emails.
    • Creating meeting summaries.
    • Organising information.
    • Producing templates.
    • Generating initial drafts.

    Confidential information must not be included unless using an approved secure system.

    4. Assessment and Feedback

    AI may support assessment processes by:

    • Generating practice questions.
    • Suggesting feedback structures.
    • Helping identify common misconceptions.

    However:

    • Teachers remain responsible for assessment decisions.
    • AI-generated feedback must be reviewed.
    • AI should not determine student outcomes independently.

    Professional judgement must always be applied.

    5. Using AI-Generated Content

    Before sharing AI-generated content, staff should check:

    Accuracy

    Does the information appear correct?

    Has it been verified using reliable sources?

    Bias

    Could the content unfairly represent:

    • Individuals?
    • Groups?
    • Cultures?
    • Viewpoints?

    Suitability

    Is the content appropriate for:

    • Student age?
    • Subject area?
    • Curriculum requirements?

    Originality

    Does the content respect:

    • Copyright?
    • Intellectual property?
    • Existing educational materials?

    6. AI and Safeguarding

    Staff should remain aware that AI systems may generate:

    • Inaccurate advice.
    • Harmful content.
    • Inappropriate responses.
    • Misleading information.

    Any safeguarding concerns arising from AI use should follow existing school safeguarding procedures.

    AI should never replace appropriate safeguarding processes or professional intervention.

    7. Supporting Student AI Literacy

    Staff have an important role in helping students develop responsible AI skills.

    Where appropriate, staff should encourage students to:

    • Question AI-generated information.
    • Verify sources.
    • Understand limitations.
    • Use AI ethically.
    • Reflect on their own learning.

    Students should understand that effective AI use requires critical thinking.

    8. AI in Different Subjects

    Departments may develop subject-specific guidance.

    Examples:

    Computer Science

    AI may be used to explore:

    • Programming assistance.
    • Algorithm development.
    • AI systems and ethics.

    Students should still demonstrate understanding of their own code and solutions.

    Humanities

    AI may support:

    • Research exploration.
    • Discussion of viewpoints.
    • Argument development.

    Students should evaluate accuracy and potential bias.

    Creative Subjects

    AI may support:

    • Idea generation.
    • Experimentation.
    • Exploration of techniques.

    Students should understand copyright and ownership considerations.

    9. Reporting Concerns

    Staff should report concerns regarding AI use, including:

    • Data breaches.
    • Unsafe AI outputs.
    • Inappropriate student use.
    • Potential academic misconduct.

    Concerns should be reported through existing school procedures.

    10. Professional Development

    The school will support staff in developing AI literacy through:

    • Training sessions.
    • Guidance updates.
    • Sharing of effective practice.
    • Evaluation of emerging technologies.

    Staff are encouraged to explore AI tools responsibly and share successful approaches.

    11. Commitment

    AI represents an opportunity to enhance education when combined with professional expertise.

    The school encourages staff to experiment responsibly with AI while maintaining high standards of teaching, safeguarding, privacy and educational quality.

    AI should make teachers more effective, not replace the knowledge, creativity and relationships that underpin excellent education.
