    Artificial Intelligence Acceptable Use Policy

    Field Details
    Document Owner Oliver Caplan
    Version 1.0
    Status Draft
    Review Frequency Annual
    Classification Public

    1. Purpose

    This policy defines the acceptable and unacceptable uses of Artificial Intelligence (AI) within the school.

    The purpose of this policy is to ensure that AI technologies are used safely, responsibly and effectively while supporting innovation and preparing students for a future where AI is increasingly integrated into education and employment.

    This policy should be read alongside:

    • AI Ethics Statement
    • Assessment AI Policy
    • Student AI Guidance
    • Staff AI Guidance
    • Data Protection Policy

    2. Scope

    This policy applies to:

    • All students
    • All teaching staff
    • All support staff
    • Any individual using AI systems through school facilities or for school-related activities

    It applies to:

    • Generative AI tools
    • AI assistants
    • AI-powered educational platforms
    • AI tools used for research, writing, programming, design or administration

    3. General Principles

    AI may be used when it:

    • Supports learning and understanding.
    • Improves accessibility.
    • Encourages creativity and exploration.
    • Increases efficiency without reducing educational quality.
    • Is used transparently and responsibly.

    AI must not be used to:

    • Avoid learning.
    • Misrepresent work.
    • Circumvent assessment requirements.
    • Create harm or deceive others.
    • Share confidential information.

    4. Student Acceptable Use

    Students may use AI for:

    Learning Support

    Examples include:

    • Asking for explanations of difficult concepts.
    • Generating examples for revision.
    • Creating practice questions.
    • Exploring alternative approaches to problems.
    • Receiving feedback on ideas or drafts.

    Creativity and Development

    Examples include:

    • Brainstorming ideas.
    • Developing creative projects.
    • Exploring programming concepts.
    • Supporting design and planning.

    Organisation

    Examples include:

    • Creating study plans.
    • Organising notes.
    • Improving personal productivity.

    5. Student Unacceptable Use

    Students must not:

    • Submit AI-generated work as their own without acknowledgement.
    • Use AI during assessments where it has been prohibited.
    • Ask AI to complete assessed work on their behalf.
    • Generate fake references, sources or evidence.
    • Use AI to impersonate another person.
    • Use AI to create harmful, offensive or inappropriate content.
    • Upload personal information about themselves or others into unauthorised AI systems.

    6. Staff Acceptable Use

    Staff may use AI to support:

    • Lesson planning.
    • Resource creation.
    • Differentiated teaching materials.
    • Administrative tasks.
    • Drafting communications.
    • Generating ideas for classroom activities.
    • Professional learning.

    7. Staff Responsibilities

    Staff using AI must:

    • Review all AI-generated content before use.
    • Ensure resources are accurate and suitable.
    • Consider potential bias or inaccuracies.
    • Protect confidential information.
    • Follow school-approved systems and procedures.

    AI-generated content should never replace professional judgement.

    8. Data Protection and Privacy

    Users must not enter:

    • Student personal data.
    • Medical information.
    • Safeguarding information.
    • Confidential school documents.
    • Passwords or security information.

    into AI systems unless explicitly authorised.

    Only approved AI tools may be used for processing school-related information.

    9. AI Accuracy and Reliability

    Users must recognise that AI systems can:

    • Produce incorrect information.
    • Generate outdated information.
    • Reflect biases present in training data.
    • Create misleading outputs.

    AI-generated information should be checked against reliable sources before being relied upon.

    10. Monitoring and Review

    The school may review AI usage to ensure compliance with:

    • School policies.
    • Data protection requirements.
    • Safeguarding responsibilities.
    • Educational standards.

    This policy will be reviewed annually or when significant changes occur in AI technology, legislation or Department for Education guidance.

    11. Commitment

    The school encourages responsible experimentation with AI while maintaining high standards of integrity, safety and respect.

    AI should be used as a tool to enhance learning, creativity and productivity while preserving the importance of independent thought and human judgement.
