    1. Introduction

    Artificial Intelligence (AI) is becoming an increasingly important part of education, employment and everyday life.

    The school recognises that AI tools can provide valuable support for learning. Students are encouraged to explore and use AI responsibly, while understanding that AI is a tool to support learning rather than a replacement for personal effort, creativity and critical thinking.

    This guidance explains how students should use AI safely and effectively.

    2. What is Artificial Intelligence?

    Artificial Intelligence refers to computer systems that can perform tasks that usually require human intelligence.

    Examples include:

    • Generating text.
    • Answering questions.
    • Producing images.
    • Writing or explaining code.
    • Analysing information.
    • Providing suggestions and feedback.

    AI systems can be powerful, but they do not think or understand in the same way as humans.

    3. Using AI for Learning

    Students are encouraged to use AI to support their learning.

    Appropriate uses include:

    Understanding Concepts

    AI can help explain difficult topics by:

    • Providing alternative explanations.
    • Giving examples.
    • Breaking complex ideas into smaller steps.
    • Helping identify areas for further study.

    Revision Support

    AI can be used to:

    • Generate practice questions.
    • Create revision summaries.
    • Test understanding.
    • Suggest revision strategies.

    Students should always check that revision materials are accurate.

    Improving Work

    AI may help students:

    • Review the structure of writing.
    • Identify weaknesses in arguments.
    • Suggest improvements.
    • Check clarity and organisation.

    The final work must remain the student's own.

    Creativity and Exploration

    AI can support:

    • Brainstorming ideas.
    • Planning projects.
    • Exploring different approaches.
    • Developing creative work.

    Students should use AI as a starting point rather than allowing it to replace their own ideas.

    4. Responsible AI Use

    When using AI, students should:

    Think critically

    AI can make mistakes.

    Always ask:

    • Is this information accurate?
    • Does this match what I have learned?
    • Can I verify this from another source?

    Be transparent

    If AI has contributed significantly to your work, follow your teacher's instructions about acknowledging its use.

    Protect information

    Do not enter:

    • Passwords.
    • Personal information.
    • Details about other students.
    • Safeguarding information.
    • Confidential school information.

    into AI systems.

    5. AI and Homework

    Teachers will provide guidance on whether AI may be used for specific tasks.

    Before using AI for homework, students should consider:

    • Is AI allowed for this task?
    • Am I using AI to learn or just to complete the work?
    • Could I explain this work without AI assistance?

    If the answer to the final question is no, the AI has probably replaced too much of the learning process.

    6. AI and Assessments

    Different assessments will have different rules.

    Students must follow teacher instructions.

    AI may be:

    Not allowed

    For tasks designed to measure independent ability.

    Examples:

    • Exams.
    • Controlled assessments.
    • Specific independent tasks.

    Allowed with acknowledgement

    For tasks where AI can support learning.

    Examples:

    • Planning.
    • Research support.
    • Feedback.

    Encouraged

    For tasks where understanding AI is part of the learning objective.

    Examples:

    • Computer science projects.
    • Digital literacy activities.

    7. What AI Should Not Be Used For

    Students must not use AI to:

    • Submit generated answers as their own.
    • Complete assessed work where AI is prohibited.
    • Create fake sources or references.
    • Copy another person's work.
    • Generate harmful or inappropriate content.
    • Impersonate another person.

    8. Making Good AI Prompts

    Good AI use requires good questions.

    A useful prompt should include:

    Context

    Explain what you are trying to do.

    Example:

    I am a Year 12 student studying economics.

    Task

    Explain what you need help with.

    Example:

    Explain the causes of inflation.

    Requirements

    Specify what type of response would help.

    Example:

    Give an explanation suitable for A Level revision.

    9. Checking AI Responses

    Before using AI-generated information, check:

    • Accuracy.
    • Sources.
    • Date of information.
    • Potential bias.
    • Whether it applies to your situation.

    Remember:

    AI can produce confident answers that are incorrect.

    10. Building Future Skills

    Understanding AI is an important part of preparing for the future.

    Students should aim to develop:

    • Digital literacy.
    • Critical thinking.
    • Creativity.
    • Ethical judgement.
    • Problem-solving skills.

    The most valuable skill is not simply knowing how to use AI, but knowing when and how to use it effectively.

    11. Support

    Students who are unsure whether AI use is appropriate should speak to:

    • Their teacher.
    • Their subject leader.
    • The school's AI Governance Group.

    The school encourages questions and responsible experimentation with AI.
